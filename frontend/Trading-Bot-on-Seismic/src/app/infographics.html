The Data Privacy Model: Public vs. Private

The core of the architecture is the on-chain data split. A traditional blockchain makes all trade data public, while Seismic hides the sensitive components.

Traditional Blockchain

All sensitive data, including your balance, entry price, and PnL, is 100% public and visible to everyone.

Seismic Confidential Blockchain

Only non-sensitive data is public. Your balance, trade size, PnL, and strategy parameters remain fully encrypted and private.

Confidential Trade Lifecycle (HTML Flowchart)

The trade process ensures privacy at every step by keeping sensitive computation inside the Confidential Smart Contract (CSC).

1. Trade Initiation (Entry)

The off-chain Decision Engine detects a signal and sends an encrypted payload (Action: BUY, Size, Slippage) to the CSC. The CSC privately verifies the Encrypted Balance, records the Encrypted Entry Price, and sets the public status to 'Active'.

2. Trade Management (Take-Profit)

The off-chain Engine monitors the public market price. It sends an encrypted query to the CSC: "Is `CurrentPrice` > `EncryptedEntryPrice` + `EncryptedProfitTarget`?" The CSC performs this comparison confidentially and returns only a simple True/False, revealing no private data.

3. Trade Finalization (Exit)

When the check is `True`, the Engine sends an Encrypted SELL instruction. The CSC confidentially calculates the Encrypted PnL, updates the Encrypted WalletBalance, and sets the public status to 'Closed'. The owner can view their private PnL later via the UI.
